# D1 – Modal verbs (can/could/must/have to/should/may/might)
> Level: newbie • Focus: khả năng / lời khuyên / bắt buộc / xin phép • Form: modal + V1

---

## Purpose
Giúp bạn diễn đạt “có thể / nên / phải / được phép” đúng sắc thái trong nói & viết (workplace, email, instructions), tránh sai form và nhầm nghĩa.

## Key points
- Modal + **V1**: `can/could/may/might/must/should + base verb` (không chia s/es/ed).
- Phủ định: `modal + not + V1` (cannot/can’t, mustn’t, shouldn’t…).
- Câu hỏi: đảo modal lên đầu: `Can you…?` / `Should we…?`
- `must` = bắt buộc mạnh/“tôi yêu cầu” (speaker obligation); `have to` = bắt buộc do luật/quy định/tình huống.
- `mustn’t` = **cấm**; `don’t have to` = **không bắt buộc** (rất hay nhầm).
- `should` = lời khuyên/điều nên làm (không mạnh như must).
- `may/might` = khả năng (might yếu hơn); `may` cũng dùng để xin phép (formal).
- `could` = lịch sự hơn `can` khi xin phép/đề nghị; cũng có thể là khả năng trong quá khứ.

## Rule summary
### 1) Form
- Affirmative: `modal + V1`
- Negative: `modal + not + V1`
- Question: `Modal + subject + V1?`

### 2) Meanings (core)
- Ability: `can` (hiện tại), `could` (quá khứ)
- Permission: `can/could/may` (could/may lịch sự hơn)
- Advice: `should`
- Obligation: `must` / `have to`
- Possibility: `may/might/could`

## Examples
### A) Ability
1. I **can** type fast.
2. When I was a kid, I **could** swim very well.

### B) Permission (asking/giving)
3. **Can** I leave early today?
4. **Could** you open the window, please? (polite request)

### C) Advice
5. You **should** back up your files regularly.
6. We **shouldn’t** delay the meeting again.

### D) Obligation
7. You **must** wear a helmet here. (rule / strong)
8. I **have to** work late tonight. (situation)

### E) Possibility
9. It **may** rain later.
10. She **might** be in a meeting now.

## Common mistakes
1. Sai: *He can plays football.* → Đúng: **He can play football.** (modal + V1)
2. Sai: *Do you can help me?* → Đúng: **Can you help me?**
3. Sai: *You mustn’t to park here.* → Đúng: **You mustn’t park here.**
4. Sai (nhầm nghĩa): *You don’t have to wear a uniform* (ý muốn nói “cấm”) → Đúng: **You mustn’t wear…** (cấm)
5. Sai: *I should to go now.* → Đúng: **I should go now.**
6. Sai: *He may can come.* → Đúng: **He may come. / He can come.** (không dùng 2 modal liền nhau)

## Practice A
> 15 câu (chọn/điền/sửa). Chọn đáp án đúng nhất.

1) She ___ speak three languages. (can / must)

2) When I was 10, I ___ run very fast. (can / could)

3) ___ I borrow your charger for a minute? (May / Must)

4) You ___ see a doctor if the pain continues. (should / might)

5) Employees ___ wear an ID badge in this building. (must / might)

6) I ___ work late today because we have a deadline. (have to / may)

7) You ___ smoke here. It’s forbidden. (mustn’t / don’t have to)

8) You ___ bring cash. We accept cards. (mustn’t / don’t have to)

9) It’s cloudy. It ___ rain later. (may / should)

10) She isn’t answering. She ___ be busy. (might / mustn’t)

11) (Fix the sentence) *He can to drive.*

12) (Fix the sentence) *Do you can help me?*

13) (Choose) ___ you please send me the file? (Could / Must)

14) We ___ start the meeting now, or we’ll be late. (should / may)

15) (Choose) You ___ be quiet in the library. (must / can)

## Answer key
1) **can** — ability
2) **could** — past ability
3) **May** — permission (formal)
4) **should** — advice
5) **must** — rule/obligation
6) **have to** — obligation due to situation
7) **mustn’t** — prohibition (cấm)
8) **don’t have to** — not necessary (không bắt buộc)
9) **may** — possibility
10) **might** — possibility (weak)
11) **He can drive.** — modal + V1
12) **Can you help me?** — modal question form
13) **Could** — polite request
14) **should** — recommended action
15) **must** — rule/obligation

## Practice B

1) Viết 3 câu về **ability**: 2 câu dùng `can`, 1 câu dùng `could` (past).

3) Viết 4 câu **lời khuyên** dùng `should/shouldn’t` (bối cảnh: sức khỏe hoặc công việc).

5) Viết 1 đoạn 5–6 câu về “company rules”, bắt buộc có: `must`, `mustn’t`, `don’t have to`, `should`.

## Self-check rubric
- Tôi luôn dùng đúng form `modal + V1` (không to, không s/es).
- Tôi phân biệt được `mustn’t` (cấm) vs `don’t have to` (không bắt buộc).
- Tôi dùng `must` vs `have to` đúng theo nguồn bắt buộc (speaker vs rule/situation).
- Tôi chọn `can/could/may` đúng độ lịch sự khi xin phép/nhờ.
- Tôi dùng `may/might` đúng nghĩa “có thể” (không chắc chắn).
